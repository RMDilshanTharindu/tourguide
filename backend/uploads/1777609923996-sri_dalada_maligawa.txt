Sri Dalada Maligawa (Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic)

Sri Dalada Maligawa, also known as the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic,
is one of the most sacred Buddhist sites in Sri Lanka. It is located in
Kandy, a historic city in the Central Province of Sri Lanka.

Historical Significance

The temple houses the sacred tooth relic of Lord Buddha, which is
considered an important symbol of Buddhist faith and Sri Lankan
heritage. According to history, the relic was brought to Sri Lanka in
the 4th century AD from India by Princess Hemamali and Prince Dantha.

Cultural Importance

Sri Dalada Maligawa is not only a religious site but also a symbol of
Sri Lankan sovereignty. Historically, whoever possessed the relic was
believed to have the divine right to rule the country.

Architecture

The temple complex showcases traditional Kandyan architecture, with
beautifully carved wooden pillars, golden roofs, and intricate
stonework. It is part of the royal palace complex of the former Kingdom
of Kandy.

Religious Practices

Daily rituals, known as Thevava, are performed three times a day: -
Morning - Noon - Evening

Devotees and visitors gather to worship and offer flowers.

Esala Perahera

One of the most famous cultural festivals associated with the temple is
the annual Esala Perahera, a grand procession featuring: - Traditional
dancers - Drummers - Decorated elephants - Fire performers

This festival attracts thousands of local and international visitors.

UNESCO Recognition

Sri Dalada Maligawa is part of the Sacred City of Kandy, which was
designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1988.

Visitor Information

-   Location: Kandy, Sri Lanka
-   Main Attraction: Sacred Tooth Relic
-   Dress Code: Modest clothing required
-   Best Time to Visit: During religious ceremonies or Esala Perahera
    season

Conclusion

Sri Dalada Maligawa remains a powerful spiritual, cultural, and
historical landmark in Sri Lanka, representing centuries of devotion,
tradition, and national identity.
