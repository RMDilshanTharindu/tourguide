The Gal Viharaya, also known as Uttararama, is a rock temple of the Buddha situated in the ancient city of Polonnaruwa in Sri Lanka. It was fashioned in the 12th century by King Parakramabahu I.

The central feature of the temple is four magnificent Buddha statues, which have been carved into the face of a large granite rock. Among these, the Standing Image is 7 metres (23 ft) tall and is located in the center. Unlike most standing Buddha statues, the arms are crossed across the chest, a gesture known as 'Paradukkha Karunitava' or sorrow for the sorrows of others.

The Reclining Image is the largest, measuring 14.12 metres (46.3 ft) long. It depicts the 'Parinirvana' of the Buddha—his final passing away. You can notice a slight depression in the pillow under the head, showing the realistic detail the 12th-century artisans achieved.

To the left of the standing image is the Vidyhadhara Guha, a small artificial cave carved into the rock. Inside is a seated Buddha image 4 feet 7 inches high, sitting on a throne decorated with lions and thunderbolts.
